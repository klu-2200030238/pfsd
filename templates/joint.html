Joint Family

Multiple Generations

One of the defining features of a joint family is that it consists of multiple generations living under one roof. This includes grandparents, parents, children, and sometimes even extended family members such as uncles, aunts, and cousins.

Shared Residences

Members of a joint family typically live together in the same household or within close proximity. This shared living arrangement fosters a sense of belonging and mutual support among family members.

Responsibilities

Members of a joint family share various responsibilities, including household chores, childcare, and financial obligations. Each member contributes to the functioning of the family unit in their own way.

Family Bonding

Living together fosters strong emotional bonds among family members. Shared experiences, celebrations, and challenges strengthen the sense of kinship and solidarity within the joint family.
